Lethbridge is located in the southern parts of the province. This area is mainly known to have lots of flat prairie and farmland, with the forested area being parks. Lethbridge has a couple of bridges built over the Old Man River, which takes you to the city's west side. The Old Man River is enclosed by coulees which are gradually rolling hills. There are 3 bridges, 2 of which are used for vehicles and 1 for trains. The train bridge is known by many as a landmark in Lethbridge. The High-Level Bridge is generally used for trains carrying coal across the river in 1909. According to an article on the High-Level Bridge by Tourism Lethbridge on April 18, 2020, "This bridge is not only the longest but also the highest steel railway trestle bridge in the world reaching over 5,327 ft in length and 314 ft in height this bridge is truly a sight to see!"

This area has hot, dry summers followed by shivering cold winters with plenty of snowfalls. Lethbridge is also known for its chinook winds that blow in a westerly direction from the Rocky Mountains.
